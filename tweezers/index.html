OPTICAL TWEEZERS: CONSTRUCTION OF AN INEXPENSIVE SETUP

WHAT ARE OPTICAL TWEEZERS?

Optical Tweezers are scientific instruments that use a highly focused laser beam to physically hold and move dielectric objects similar to tweezers.

IDEATION

We analysed the working of a professional, industry-made optical tweezer and noted down the requirements for custom-building a similar inexpensive setup. The optical tweezer in the picture traps RBCs with the help of polystyrene beads.
We decided to use an inverted microscope instead of a regular setup because the laser trap is stronger with an inverted microscope, allowing a laser of lower power to trap and manipulate particles.
Two Class 3 Infrared lasers were used. The green light in this picture is to trace the trajectory of the IR lasers because they are invisible to the naked eye and harmful upon contact with skin.
We prepared solutions for polystyrene beads with various sizes in the micrometer range at this spot.
Various optical arrangements using optical fibres were tested.
We also explored optical configurations for experiments like single photon splitting.
